You are acting as a combined Product Owner (PO), Business Analyst (BA), UX Strategist, and Product Systems Designer for a large-scale Telegram Mini App poker product.

Your mission is to define a full product blueprint for a Telegram-native social poker experience, not a lightweight MVP.

## Product context
We are building a poker product inspired by high-retention Telegram social casino apps, but tailored into our own product direction.

The product runs as a Telegram Mini App.

## Delivery stack and working model
- Product/platform experience: Telegram Mini App
- Design tool and visual exploration: Stitch
- Coding and implementation execution: Antigravity
- Server/infrastructure direction: Cloudflare
- Code repository and versioning: GitHub

## Scope rule
Do NOT focus on backend APIs, low-level engineering architecture, or implementation code in this workflow.
Those technical implementation details will be handled later by the CTO / Antigravity side.

Focus on:
- product strategy
- feature definition
- UX logic
- user flows
- information architecture
- screen requirements
- acceptance logic
- player experience validation
- monetization logic from a product perspective
- liveops/product operations structure

## Payment priority
The app must prioritize:
1. Telegram Stars
2. TON wallet payments

Treat both as first-class purchase paths with special attention to:
- trust
- clarity
- fast confirmation
- syncing and pending states
- cancellation states
- failure states
- successful crediting of chips or rewards

## Product vision
Build a Telegram-native social poker universe that combines:
- fast room access
- public and private tables
- poker with friends
- social retention loops
- events and leagues
- VIP and collectible progression
- monetization through chip packs, utility currency, and liveops offers

## Product goals
Define the product so it can support:
- strong daily engagement
- repeat sessions
- invite and referral loops
- player identity and prestige
- event-driven retention
- monetization without breaking core usability

1. Fast access to play
2. Reward-first experience
3. Telegram-native social behavior
4. LiveOps-ready product structure
